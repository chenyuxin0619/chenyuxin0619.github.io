Research Proposal

The COVID-19 pandemic has reshaped the world in many ways: students switched to distance learning, labour switched to WFH, local restaurants rely on delivery services (Uber Eats, Door Dash), and famous brands use online promotion more heavily. All mentioned above are based on the Internet which has developed for decades, yet I am curious about where our next step is: Can our Internet experience be more immersive and interactive? Previous studies indicated that Metaverse could be the solution. But how far are we from an all-in-one and affordable metaverse experience? My research interest is to combine technologies like Web 3.0, VR, AR, and Blockchain to provide the public with a new way to study, work and entertain.

The Metaverse is the post-reality universe, a perpetual and persistent multiuser environment merging physical reality with digital virtuality, the metaverse is based on virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR). (Mystakidis, 2023) Vishwakarma, Shaikh, and Yadav (2023) also agreed that Metaverse is the new future, a new world. Currently, there is an intense race to construct the infrastructure, protocols, and standards that will govern the Metaverse. Large corporations are striving to construct their closed, proprietary hardware and software ecosystems to attract users and become the de facto Metaverse destination. For example, Facebook was renamed Meta years ago, and the leading Chinese technology company, Bite Dance, is investing heavily in Metaverse. Yet which country is dominating this research field? Some may argue that it must be the United States of America while others argue that non-English speaking counties like Spain, China, and Indian in fact lead our way in this round of metaverse revolution. López-Belmonte, Pozo-Sánchez, Moreno-Guerrero, and Lampropoulos (2023) did systematic research about metaverse in education in their paper. They use the open database and do a literature review to answer a series of questions including What have been the most prominent countries in the production of the educational metaverse? What have been the most relevant objectives in the documents on the educational metaverse? What research methodologies have been used in studies on the educational metaverse? and What have been the main findings analyzed in research on the educational metaverse? López-Belmonte et al. (2023) state that a predominance of publications in Brazil and Spanish-speaking countries (Spain, Mexico, and Colombia) has been confirmed. This fact reflects a lack of scientific literature in English-speaking and Asian countries, which usually reflects high levels of production in pioneering research in educational contexts.

We see the public interested in the idea of a metaverse which is proved by the success of the game “Animal Crossing”: when the city is locked down, friends and families can still arrange parties in the game. JP Morgan (2022) has also drafted a blueprint of opportunities regarding metaverse for us: 1) People spend $54 billion on virtual goods every year (this is almost double the amount people spend on music) 2) Approximately 60 billion messages are sent daily on Roblox. 3) More than $80 million USD was paid to the creator. 4) NFTs currently have a market cap of 41 billion US dollars. We also see the metaverse era coming into the academic circle, Damar (2021) suggests till 2021, there are 42 articles produced about the metaverse and these articles are cited 217 times. Bale, Ghorpade, Hashim, Vaishnav, and Almaspoor (2022) state in their comprehensive study on the Metaverse that the metaverse contributes to Experienced Virtual Tours, Meetings and Conferences, Workouts and Exercise, Education, Training, E-commerce, and Trade of Digital Assets.

However, like many new ideas, opportunities are born with risks. Metaverse platforms like Meta and Sandbox are launching explosively, however, we must be aware of the potential bubble inherited from it. Vidal-Tom ́as (2023) states that we see the explosive launch of metaverse tokens in past years. However, with the explosive behaviour of the native currencies in all the metaverse, and the influence of the tokens on the NFT price, the potential risk exists and the metaverse is only an illusion. Personally, I believe NFTs are still in their infant stage which lacks government regulation, and the current NFT market has moral hazard and adverse selection problems. And from the social perspective, scientists should focus on the long-run benefit rather than focus on the profit from speculations. Vishwakarma et al. (2023) state that privacy and security are also other important factors to be a concern when entering the metaverse, especially when trading in the metaverse, due to the current limitations of VR and its technology, users are not able to fully recognize their surroundings in the metaverse and who they are.

Said (2023) used a sampling model in his research and concluded that the Metaverse will have a revolutionary impact on the future of electronic and hybrid learning and training. Mystakidis (2023) argued that educational institutions can benefit a lot from the metaverse: imagine that a high school teacher is showing the class how life in ancient Egypt was, then students put their VR headsets on, and suddenly they all travel back to ancient Egypt. The experiment design from Said has inspired me that I must take demographic features into consideration when creating my own design. For example, if I were to study the level of acceptance of education in the metaverse, I should limit my respondent to those who were mature enough to use VR headsets.

In 2021 Zuckerberg introduced the metaverse office concept. Metaverse office refers to the ability to allow users to customize a digital avatar, let companies set up their metaverse office, and enable communication between members through virtual space. Chen (2023) argued that technology provides companies with low-cost office space, flexible work options for employees, and reduced carbon emissions and energy consumption. Compared to traditional work methods, metaverse office combines flexibility and low cost, which is why some companies choose this model. Yet a cost and benefit analysis is necessary before promoting the metaverse office to the massive public. Currently, we see some shortcomings in the metaverse office, for example, salespersons still prefer face-to-face conversation over meetings in the metaverse. In addition, companies may consider the sunk cost of implementing metaverse office as the devices are still very pricy. Thus, when promoting the metaverse office we could introduce a rent-to-own method.

E-commerce is also a crucial part of the metaverse. Currently, the trade volume from e-commerce is increasing each year, this has directly created many wonderful companies including Alibaba, Tiktok, and Amazon. People are betting on the metaverse business for the future. Utoyo, Warbung, Bonafix, Aorilla and Oei (2022) in their research answered the question of why big brand advertising in the metaverse. Periyasami and Periyasamy (2022) did a case study on metaverse as a future promising platform business model and found the key challenge to be currency and trust. Cryptocurrency is gaining popularity all around the world, and it will be much more important in the ecology of a metaverse if it ever becomes one. In addition to that, blockchain technology and cryptocurrencies have shown several noteworthy benefits over traditional forms of the financial business. Manufacturing and enterprises of different destinations need to re‐look at their strategy. Utoyo, Warbung, Bonafix, Aorilla and Oei (2022) answered why big brand advertising is in the metaverse.

The entire research process will take about 7-8 months. To finish my research, I would use a questionnaire and use the result from the questionnaire to proceed with regression analysis. I will start the questionnaire design at the beginning of my PhD program as it is the most time-consuming part of this research. Also, I must admit that my vision is limited thus I need to discuss it with my friends, classmates, and professors to ensure the quality of the questionnaire. After handing out the questionnaire, I will start my literature review before creating the regression model. When we get the results from the questionnaire, we should also have studied enough academic papers as preliminary research. This first stage will take about 3 months. The second stage consists of regression data collection/cleaning, model creation, and regression analysis. In this stage, the most time-consuming part is data collection which takes about 2-3 months. Note that the model would need peer review to ensure its quality. A first draft follows the end of the second stage. The third stage consists of a more detailed regression and questionnaire analysis. When finished, another peer view is needed before finishing the final draft. This whole process takes about 1 month. The last stage takes about 1 month to complete the final writing and review and prepare the academic presentation.
